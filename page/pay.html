如您常用的銀行賬戶不是開立在香港，您可以選用當地銀行的國際匯款服務。有些國家或地區對國際匯款有一定的限制，建議您在使用此服務前先向銀行詢問有關細節。請注意，我們不接受通過「西聯匯款」（Western Union）的存款。

郵局匯款（只限居住於中國大陸的客戶）
中國地區的客戶可以使用國內郵政局提供的「銀郵匯款」或稱為「一匯通」的國際匯款服務，使用郵局電匯每個月的存款上限為1萬美元。

處理時間
一般需時1-5個工作天，如果使用同一集團的銀行服務，速度可能會加快，手續費也會比較便宜，例如從中國銀行匯款至香港中銀；或者是台灣的滙豐銀行匯款至香港的滙豐銀行。

手續費
各家銀行的收費不太一樣，詳情可以直接聯絡您的導出銀行，不過我們舉2個例子方便您有個概念：

1. 舉中國銀行為例，銀行收取的費用包括銀行匯款手續費（匯款金額的1%，最低50元，最高260元人民幣）和電訊費；150元人民幣（港澳地區80元）；

2.舉台灣國披世華銀行為例，銀行收取的費用約台幣400元，網上銀行會便宜一點，約台幣300元。

提醒您：以上的收費可能不包括中轉銀行的費用。國際銀行間電匯轉賬，一般而言，國外中間轉賬銀行會自匯款款項中扣取處理費，收款行一般也會另收取匯入匯款手續費，因此收款人實際收到之款項金額，可能與原來之匯款金額不同。

Remittance through the Post Office (only for customers residing in mainland China) Customers in China can use the "Yinyou Remittance" which is also called “Yihuitong” services that provided by the domestic Postal Office to conduct international remittance. The maximum deposit limit for each month using the post office telegraphic transfer services is $10,000.

Handling time
It usually takes 1-5 working days. If you use banking services from the same group, the speed may be faster and the handling fees will be cheaper. For example, you transfer the money from Bank of China to Bank of China in Hong Kong Bank of China; or from HSBC in Taiwan to HSBC in Hong Kong.

Handling fee
The charges for each bank are not the same. For details, you can directly contact the bank you want to transfer money from. However, here we give two examples to give you a clearer picture:

1. Let’s take the Bank of China as an example. The fees charged by the bank include the handling fee for remittance (1% of the remittance amount, and the minimum is 50 yuan, the maximum is 260 yuan) and the telecommunication fee which is 150 yuan (80 yuan in Hong Kong and Macao);

2. Let’s take Cathay United Bank in Taiwan as another example. The bank charges about NT$400, and online banking will be a little cheaper which is about NT$300.

Reminder: the above charges may not include the fees of the intermediary bank. Generally speaking, when the telegraphic transfer is carried out between international Banks, the foreign intermediate transfer Banks will deduct processing fee from the remittance. And the receiving bank will generally charge an additional handling fee for the remittance. Therefore, the amount actually received by the payee may be different from the original remittance amount.
